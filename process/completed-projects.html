Cat Robot

Vision : My cat, BMO, eats my plants and socks. I decided to try to solve this problem by keeping her entertained elsewhere, and redirecting her attention. A toy has never managed to survive BMOâ€™s grasps, so I knew it would have to be a laser.

First Iteration: I wanted the toy to turn on when the object detected her in front of it and I realized I would need a sensor of some kind. As I was researching sonic detection versus PIR I found out that sonic sensors have trouble seeing cats because of their fur. Although the PIR sensor seemed like the obvious choice, it presented it's own set of issues due to it being quite a finicky piece of hardware.

When trying to decide how to project the laser out onto the floor I ended up opting to make a turret like design with a PIR sensor at the front perpendicular a my servo motor with the laser taped to it.

Issues: This was my first time woodworking, and soldering so there was a lot of mistakes to be made and a lot to be learned. On the software side of things, coding the motor, laser, and sensor to work in unsion together was a bit of a struggle. Ultimately my biggest issue with my design was how top heavy the tower was, it was easy for my little gremlin to knock it over and effectively pull out all of the wires from the arduino

Second Iteration: In my second take, in designing the enclosure there were a couple critiques I wanted to address. Mainly, the stability and having better protection for the actual laser from the cat who might try to eat it. This was a bit more difficult so I switched up my enclosure.

I hacked a laser I had found at the thrift store and decided to keep the motor and the actual light. It used the enclosure and used a mirror to reflect it out to the cat. In terms of coding, DC1 was relatively simple: the code just connected the laser to a servo and made it move in certain angles. Since I upgraded to a DC motor in DC2 it was a great opportunity to make things more interactive for the cat. Now, the rotation changes and is random as the cat plays more ( using the random() function!! ). The new motor also enables more motion (around and up and down). I feel a lot better with the final product, but as I went through the redesigning process there was so many things I wish I had extra time / resources to re-do.
