-- ana's fall 2023 independent study --

focus: translating digital media to physical medium through print

topics of interest:
- creating subversive basics
- printing on fabrics + clothing
- making unique household decor
- printing posters
- working with inks

creating digital imagery :

I wanted all of the prints that I made for my independent study to be things I either hand drew, or digitally composed. I spent a couple weeks making lots of different designs for different purposes. At some point I realized I needed to shift towards designs that could be easily printed or stamped.

poster prints:

these were made for my wall! printed on a medium format printer

printing on wood :

I actually didn't plan to do this initially, but wanted to test the capabilities of our laser cutter in the lab so I could eventually make layered paper posters or stamps to make prints. In my first round of tests the image in photoshop then bringing it into rhino
Initially I had tried making only vector images in Illustrator, but the laser cutter actually did a great job of reading pngs
I printed on a couple different items at a few different sizes. My final print took about an hour on the machine to complete .

" of sappho: "

This piece is based on a poem by Sappho, one of the first recognized queer poets in writers from ancient Greece. The term sapphic comes from her name, and the word lesbian comes from the island she lived on, Lesbos.

block prints:

This was by far my favorite printing method, I started by laser cutting rubber pieces multiple times over with the same design to get some depth and some variation
My first stamp that worked! Printed on cardstock using archive ink, to test for what was about to happen... I actually ended up liking this method and would come to adopt it later on.

printing on fabric:

This was part of my home decor plan, that I had layed out in my initial document when we were planning out the independent study. A novel idea sure, but in reality I think I might need to do some more research on fabrics and inks and what is best suited for what.
american dream printed on old curtains
tv girls song printed on a tie!

making band prints + shirts:

It was at this point I realized that I could use the methods that I had previously been playing with to make something I had been needing-- band merch! We had been wanting to make tshirts and potentially other items so I set off to it.
I hand drew a picture for the stamp, then made an image on photoshop, flipped it and cut it into another one of my rubber blocks.
Testing printing on fabric
first good fabric prints... victory!

-- reflections --

the experience:

This experience has been so much more than just an academic endeavor; it's genuinely become a passion that I'm excited to continue growing.I'm so grateful I was given the time and the space to do this project. I wasn't really sure what it would look like when I started, and had started to feel nervous because I wasn't able to access many of the different equipment I had initially planned. but, I think because I used what was around me in abundance I was actually able to have way more flexibility and time to experiment, mess up and make things I really liked.


moving forward:

I plan on continuing the work that I've been doing in my independent study into the next semester and even beyond that. The process has been incredibly rewarding and enjoyable for me. Creating and experimenting with new ideas was a significant learning journey. I want to keep exploring the limits of what I can do, and how much further I can push my creativity and technical skills. Every print has been a step, and I feel like I've just caught my stride. There's so much more I want to learn!
